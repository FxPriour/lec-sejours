Reading — A World Cup across three countries

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the biggest football tournament ever held — for the first time, it is being co-hosted by three countries: the United States, Mexico, and Canada. The tournament runs from 11 June to 19 July, with 48 teams competing across 104 matches.

England are in Group L, alongside Croatia, Ghana and Panama. Captained by Harry Kane — playing in his third World Cup as captain — England are hoping to end a long wait for a major trophy. France are in Group I, with Senegal, Iraq and Norway, and are aiming to reclaim the title they won in 2018.

Because the matches are played in North America, kick-off times for European viewers are often late in the evening — sometimes even after midnight. This matters for you this summer: several matches fall during your stay with your host families, and you might be invited to watch a game together!
Adapted from FIFA / England Football / BBC Sport, June 2026

Geography — Untangling the British Isles

★ The great British geography muddle
These words are often confused — even by English speakers! Read the explanations, then complete the map on the separate sheet.
Key terms
The British Isles — a group of islands including Great Britain, Ireland, and many smaller islands. A geographical term, not political.

Great Britain (GB) — the large island containing England, Scotland, and Wales.

The United Kingdom (UK) — the political union of England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. Full name: "the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland".

Ireland — a separate island, divided into the Republic of Ireland (an independent country) and Northern Ireland (part of the UK).

England — one nation within the UK. London is its capital — and the UK's capital too.
