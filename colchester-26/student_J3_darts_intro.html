Reading

Darts is one of Britain's most beloved pub games. Its origins are disputed, but most historians believe soldiers in the Middle Ages threw short arrows at the bottom of a wine barrel to pass the time — and the game gradually evolved from there. By the 20th century, it had become a fixture of British pub culture.

Today, darts is one of the fastest-growing sports in the world. The PDC World Darts Championship, held every December at Alexandra Palace in London, attracts sell-out crowds and millions of television viewers. Professional players like Phil "The Power" Taylor and Michael van Gerwen have become household names.

What makes darts distinctly British? The connection to pub culture, the working-class roots, the noise, the pints of beer, and the passionate crowd. Unlike most sports, darts welcomes players of all ages and body types — it is a sport of precision and nerve, not physical strength.
Adapted from BBC Sport / PDC Darts
