Reading

Colchester Castle is one of the largest Norman keeps in Europe. It was built in the 11th century by the Normans — but not on empty ground. The Normans chose to construct their fortress on the foundations of the Roman Temple of Claudius, one of the most important temples in Roman Britain. They even reused Roman tiles and bricks in the walls. You can still spot them today if you look carefully.

The castle has had many lives. It served as a royal palace, a prison, and later fell into disrepair. In the 17th century, a local entrepreneur tried to demolish it to sell the materials — but the walls were so solid that he gave up. Today it is a museum, home to one of the finest collections of Roman artefacts in Britain.

Standing inside the castle, you are standing in at least three different centuries at once: Roman foundations beneath your feet, Norman stone around you, and Victorian restoration above you.
Adapted from Colchester Museums / Colchester Castle visitor materials
