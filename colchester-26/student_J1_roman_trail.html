Reading — The Roman Trivia Trail leaflet

Stop 1 — Roman Mosaic (Firstsite) Discovered in 1923 as the floor of a Roman dining room, this mosaic spent years on display at the Castle before returning to its original location, now showcased inside the Firstsite Gallery.

Stop 2 — Colchester Castle The Castle is Norman but built on the ruins of a Roman Temple, partially of Roman tiles. At 950 years old, it is less than half the age of the Roman Wall!

Stop 3 — Charles Gray's Summer House Charles Gray once owned the land that is now Castle Park. He was given the Castle as a wedding present. This summer house was built in 1731 to resemble a Roman Temple.

Stop 4 — Roman House You can see the outline of a Roman House near the Castle Park bandstand. There are no ruins as the bricks from these houses were used to build the Castle!

Stop 5 — Outer Temple Wall Don't miss the small brick wall on your left as you exit the Park. It's the last remaining piece of the Roman Temple of Claudius!

Stop 6 — Roman Theatre Peer through the window to see a section of the Roman Theatre. If you look at the paving stones nearby you can see the shape of the theatre marked out in a darker colour.

Stop 7 — Queen Boudica As you reach the Town Hall, look up at the statues. Boudica is famous for leading a rebellion against the Romans and destroying the Roman city of Camulodunum.

Stop 8 — Elephants Colchester is full of elephants! It is said the Roman emperor Claudius rode elephants into Colchester — the first elephants ever seen in Britain!

Stop 9 — Mercury Theatre Named after the Roman Messenger God, Mercury, who had wings on his feet. Inside is a replica of an important Roman artefact found in Colchester.

Stop 10 — Balkerne Gate This is the largest standing Roman Gateway left in Britain. There would have been an even larger gateway next to this, where Emperor Claudius would have arrived with his elephants.

Stop 11 — Roman Church It's thought that this might have been the very first Christian church in Britain — but it may also have been a smaller temple to a Roman God called Mithras.

Stop 12 — Roman Circus This is the only known Roman Chariot Racing site in Britain. At its height, up to eight horse-drawn chariots would have raced around the circuit.
Source: Visit Colchester — Roman Trivia Trail · visitcolchester.com

Modal verbs of deduction — talking about the past
ModalMeaningExample from the leaflet
might have beenpossible (uncertain)"It might have been the first Christian church."
may have beenpossible (slightly more certain)"It may have been a temple to Mithras."
would haveexpected or imagined past"Eight chariots would have raced around the circuit."
must have beennear certainty"It must have been an impressive sight."
