Day 1 — Session 1 · Who am I?

Tuesday 16 June · Theme: Identity · Grammar: present simple, questions

Warm-up — The name game

★ Round the class
Say your first name + an adjective that starts with the same letter and describes you.
e.g. "I'm Lively Léa." · "I'm Brave Baptiste."

Then go round again: repeat your neighbour's name and add your own.

Grammar focus

Present simple + questions
StatementYes/No questionWh- question
I live in Lyon.Do you live in Paris?Where do you live?
She has a dog.Does she have a cat?What does she have?
I am 15.Are you 16?How old are you?

Tasks

★ Task 1 — My identity card
Fill in this card about yourself. Key words are fine — no need for full sentences yet.
Name:
Age:
I come from:
I live with:
My hobbies:
I love:
I don't like:
My dream job:
★★ Task 2 — Interview your partner
Use your identity card to interview the person next to you. Ask at least 6 questions and note their answers below — you'll need them for the next task.
My partner's name: ___________________
★ Use the question help sheet if you need it.
★★★ Try indirect questions: "Could you tell me what you like to do at weekends?"
★★ Task 3 — Spot the difference
Compare your card with your partner's. Write 4 sentences showing differences or similarities. Use the words below.
Useful connectors
whereas / while (contrast) · both of us (similarity) · unlike me (difference) · just like me (similarity)
★ Simple sentences are fine: "I like football but he likes tennis."
★★★ Use whereas/while with a more complex structure.
★★★ Task 4 — Class presentation
Each pair presents their partner to the class in 3–4 sentences. No reading from your notes — just a quick glance if needed.
Suggested structure
"This is [name]. He/She is [age] and comes from [place]."
"He/She likes [hobbies] and dreams of [dream]."
"What surprised me about him/her is that…"
★★ First two sentences only.
★ You may read from your card.

Day 1 — Session 2 · Meet & Greet

Tasks

★★ Task 1 — Host family roleplay
Groups of 3. Read your role card, then act out the scene. Swap roles after each round.
🏠 Host family member
You are welcoming a French student for the first time. Be friendly and curious. Ask about:
  • their journey (Was it long? Are you tired?)
  • their family at home
  • their hobbies
  • what they want to see in England
★★★ Also ask what they find difficult about English.
✈ French student
You have just arrived. Be polite, a bit tired, but enthusiastic. Make sure to:
  • introduce yourself clearly
  • thank the family
  • ask at least 2 questions back
  • use phrases from your introduction card
★ You may keep your introduction card in front of you.
👀 Observer
Watch the conversation. After 3 minutes, give feedback:
  • One thing they did well: ______________________
  • One thing they could improve: ______________________
  • Did they ask questions back? Yes / No
★★ Task 2 — The forbidden words game
Groups of 4. One person picks a topic from the list. Describe it without using the word in bold. The others guess.
pet · guitar · shy · football · summer · nervous
★ You may mime as well as speak.
