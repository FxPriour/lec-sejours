★ Reading — Green spaces and wellbeing (15 min)
Britain has a long tradition of public parks and green spaces. In the Victorian era, city planners believed that access to nature was essential for the mental and physical health of urban populations. Today, scientists agree: studies show that spending just 20 minutes in a green space significantly reduces stress levels.

But green spaces are under threat. Urban expansion, climate change, and budget cuts have led to the loss of parks and wild areas across the UK. Campaigners argue that local authorities must protect these spaces — not only for human wellbeing, but for biodiversity. "We cannot afford to lose what little nature remains in our cities," said one environmental activist in a recent BBC report.

Crazy golf courses, like the one you are visiting today, might seem frivolous — but they are part of the green space tradition. They bring people outdoors, away from screens, and into contact with nature. In a small way, they are part of the answer to our mental health crisis.
Adapted from BBC News / The Guardian / Public Health England
Comprehension (rapide — 10 min) :
  • ★ What do scientists say about green spaces and stress?
  • ★★ Find the modal verb in the activist's quote. What does it express (obligation, possibility, advice)?
  • ★★★ The last paragraph links crazy golf to mental health. Do you find this argument convincing? Why / why not?
