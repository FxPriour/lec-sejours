★★ Reading — Animation as art (15 min)
When Toy Story was released in 1995, it changed cinema forever. It was the first feature film to be made entirely using computer-generated imagery, and it proved that animation could tell stories just as powerful as any live-action film. The film was directed by John Lasseter and produced by Pixar Animation Studios, a company that had been founded by Steve Jobs after he left Apple.

Over the following decades, the Toy Story franchise became one of the most successful in Hollywood history. The films explore themes of friendship, loyalty, growing up, and letting go — themes that resonate with adults as much as with children. Critics have argued that Toy Story 3, in which Andy goes to university and gives away his toys, is one of the most emotionally powerful films of the 21st century.

Toy Story 5, which you are about to see, continues the story. Whether it lives up to its predecessors remains to be seen — but the franchise has consistently managed to surprise audiences who thought they knew exactly what to expect.
Adapted from The Guardian / Variety

🔧 Grammar — Expressing and reporting opinions
Direct opinionHedged opinion (more nuanced)
"The film is brilliant.""I think the film is brilliant." / "The film seems brilliant."
"It was too long.""I felt it was too long." / "It could have been shorter."
"The ending was predictable.""In my opinion, the ending was somewhat predictable."
Reported speech for reviews :
"The film is brilliant." → Critics said the film was brilliant.
"I loved the animation." → She said she had loved the animation.
★★★ : "Critics have argued that…" / "Pixar has been praised for…" (passive + reporting verb)

★★ Task — Film review pitch (25 min)
En groupes de 3–4, rédigez et présentez une critique du film en 90 secondes. Structure :
Structure de la critique orale
1. Hook (une phrase accrocheuse pour commencer)
2. Summary (2–3 phrases sur l'histoire — sans tout révéler)
3. Strong point (ce qui fonctionne le mieux et pourquoi)
4. Weak point or surprise (quelque chose d'inattendu)
5. Verdict (note sur 5, recommandation)
★ A1/A2
3 phrases + une note. "Toy Story 5 is about… I liked… I didn't like… I give it __/5."
★★★ B2
Utilise au moins un verbe de discours indirect ("The director suggests that…"), un passif ("The film was praised for…") et un hedged opinion.
Vote de la classe après toutes les présentations : quelle est la meilleure critique ?
