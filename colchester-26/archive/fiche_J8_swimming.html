★ Reading — Swimming: the perfect sport? (20 min)
Swimming is often described as the ideal form of exercise. Unlike running or team sports, it works every major muscle group simultaneously, puts minimal stress on joints, and can be practised at any age. Studies show that regular swimmers have lower rates of heart disease, better sleep quality, and reduced levels of anxiety and depression.

In the UK, swimming is the most popular physical activity after walking. An estimated 2.5 million adults swim regularly, and the sport has produced some of Britain's most celebrated athletes — including Adam Peaty, who broke the 100-metre breaststroke world record nine times.

But swimming also carries risks. Drowning is one of the leading causes of accidental death worldwide, with around 320,000 fatalities each year according to the World Health Organisation. Most drowning incidents do not involve rough seas or strong currents — they happen in swimming pools, rivers, and lakes, often close to the shore, and often involve people who could swim.

The key factors in water safety are awareness, supervision, and knowing your limits. Lifeguards save lives — but they cannot watch everyone at once. Swimmers must take responsibility for their own safety and that of others.
Adapted from NHS / Sport England / World Health Organisation

★ Bridge — Safety briefing for this afternoon (10 min)
⚠ Safety rules for this afternoon — read carefully
  • You must tell the teacher immediately if you cannot swim or feel unwell.
  • You must follow the lifeguard's instructions at all times.
  • You must not run on the poolside.
  • You must not push anyone into the water.
  • You should stay in the area assigned to your group.
  • You should tell a supervisor immediately if you see someone in difficulty.
  • You do not have to swim if you feel uncomfortable — you can watch from the poolside.
  • Snorkels, masks, and flotation devices are not permitted during this session.
Quick check : each student reads one rule aloud and translates it into French. The group confirms the translation is correct.
This is not just a language exercise -- make sure every student has understood every rule before leaving for the pool.
