★ Reading — Cambridge: a city of knowledge (20 min)
Cambridge is one of the most famous university cities in the world. The University of Cambridge was founded in 1209 — making it the second oldest university in the English-speaking world, after Oxford. Today it is consistently ranked among the top three universities globally.

The university is made up of 31 colleges, each with its own traditions, buildings, and history. King's College Chapel, built between 1446 and 1515, is considered one of the finest examples of Gothic architecture in England. The Fitzwilliam Museum, founded in 1816, houses one of the greatest art and antiquities collections in Britain — and entry is free.

Cambridge is also famous for punting — gliding along the River Cam on a flat-bottomed boat. Students and tourists alike are taken along the "Backs", the famous stretch of river behind the colleges, where the gardens and medieval buildings are reflected in the water.

The city has produced 121 Nobel Prize winners — more than most countries in the world.
Adapted from University of Cambridge / Visit Cambridge

🔧 Grammar — Le passif
ActifPassifUsage
Henry VI founded King's College in 1441.King's College was founded in 1441.On met en avant l'objet, pas l'agent.
They built the chapel over 70 years.The chapel was built over 70 years.L'agent est inconnu ou sans importance.
Tourists visit Cambridge every year.Cambridge is visited by tourists every year."by" + agent si on veut le mentionner.
Formation : be (conjugué au bon temps) + participe passé
Présent : is/are + pp · Prétérit : was/were + pp · Present perfect : has/have been + pp
★★★ : passif + modal → "The chapel can be visited for free." / "The museum should be explored carefully."
