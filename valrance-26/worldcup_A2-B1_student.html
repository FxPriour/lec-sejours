Reading — A World Cup across three countries

The 2026 World Cup is the biggest ever. For the first time, three countries are hosting it together: the United States, Mexico and Canada. There are 48 teams — more than ever before — and 104 matches in total. The games are played in 16 cities: 11 in the USA, 3 in Mexico and 2 in Canada.

The group stage finished on 27 June. Now the tournament is in the knockout stage: if you lose, you go home. This week you are watching the Round of 32 and the start of the Round of 16. Every match has a winner — there are no draws any more!
Adapted from FIFA / BBC Sport, June 2026

Reading — Win or go home

In the knockout stage, every match must have a winner. If the two teams have the same score after 90 minutes, they play 30 more minutes. This is called extra time. If the score is still equal after extra time, the match is decided by a penalty shoot-out: each team takes five penalties, and the team that scores more goes through.

A penalty shoot-out is very exciting — and very stressful! Famous players sometimes miss, and goalkeepers can become heroes. Many fans say it is the most dramatic moment in football.
Adapted from the FIFA Laws of the Game, 2026

Geography — Untangling the British Isles

★ The great British geography muddle
These words are often confused — even by English speakers! Read, then answer.
Key terms
The British Isles — a group of islands: Great Britain, Ireland and many smaller ones. A geographical word, not political.

Great Britain (GB) — the large island with England, Scotland and Wales.

The United Kingdom (UK) — the political union of England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland.

Ireland — a separate island: the Republic of Ireland (independent) and Northern Ireland (part of the UK).

England — one nation inside the UK. Its capital, London, is also the UK's capital.
