Set B · Four Corners — How sure are you? (Day 2)
A2–B1 wording. Name an upcoming match; pupils stand by their level of certainty and predict aloud. (For B1–B2 use Set B-bis below.)
DEFINITELY
"They will win."
PROBABLY
"They are going to win."
MAYBE
"They might win."
NO WAY
"They won't win."
Set B-bis · Four Corners — Calibrated certainty (Day 2 · B1–B2)
Pupils place themselves and phrase a prediction whose language matches the corner.
LIKELY
"They are likely to win." / "They may well win."
POSSIBLE
"They could win." / "They might just do it."
UNLIKELY
"I doubt they'll win." / "They're unlikely to."
NO IDEA
"It's impossible to call."

Set D · "Would You Rather" — football question bank
Read one aloud; pupils walk to their side of the room and justify. Levels marked — pick to suit the group.
A1–A2
Would you rather win 1–0 or win 5–4?
Frame: "I think ___ is better because…"
A1–A2
Would you rather support the favourite or the underdog?
A1–A2
Would you rather watch the match at the stadium or on TV?
A1–A2
Would you rather be a striker or a goalkeeper?
A2–B1
Would you rather win on penalties or lose 3–0?
Use will / might to justify your choice.
A2–B1
Would you rather your team scored a lucky goal or played beautifully and drew?
A2–B1
Would you rather host the World Cup in your country or win it abroad?
A2–B1
Would you rather take the last penalty or save the last penalty?
B1–B2
Would you rather your team won ugly or lost beautifully?
Build a 30-second case; expect a rebuttal.
B1–B2
Would you rather abolish penalty shoot-outs or keep them, knowing they can feel like a lottery?
B1–B2
Would you rather a 48-team World Cup with more upsets, or the old 32-team version with higher quality?
B1–B2
Would you rather every region had its own national team, or only sovereign states?
Links to the Day 3 four-nations debate.
Tip: for "Vote with your feet", any of these also works as a two-sided split. For a quick energiser, read a statement and have pupils move to "agree / disagree" before arguing.
