World Cup 2026 — Domaine de Valrance A1–A2

Séjour 28 June – 4 July 2026 · 5 short sessions (~30–40 min each) · Knockout-stage focus (Round of 32 → Round of 16)

Day 1 — The World Cup now

Theme: host countries & cities · Grammar: present simple, numbers
🎲 Move & speak — 2 games to start
1 · Four Corners — countries — Label three corners USA / Mexico / Canada (+ one "I don't know" corner). Say a host city; pupils run to the right corner.
No prep. Re-uses the city → country sorting.
2 · Vote with your feet — true / false — One side TRUE, one side FALSE. Read simple facts ("There are 48 teams."). Pupils move and say the answer.
Keep facts very short and concrete.

Warm-up — Talk in pairs

  1. Do you like football?
  2. What is your favourite team?
  3. Point to one country on a map: USA, Mexico or Canada.

Reading — Three countries

The 2026 World Cup is very big. Three countries have the World Cup this year: the USA, Mexico and Canada. There are 48 teams. They play in 16 cities.

Now the best teams play. When you lose, you go home. There is always a winner.
Easy English — June 2026
  • ★ How many countries?
  • ★ How many teams?
  • ★★ What happens when a team loses?
Answers
3 countries · 48 teams · when you lose you go home (you are out).

Day 2 — Win or go home

Theme: how a knockout match works · Grammar: "will" for predictions
🎲 Move & speak — 2 games to start
1 · Would You Rather — football choices — Two simple options ("Win 1–0 or win 5–4?"). Pupils walk to their side and say "I think ___ is better."
Drill the frame chorally first.
2 · Vote with your feet — yes / no — "Penalties are exciting — yes or no?" Pupils move and give one word or a short reason.
Accept very short answers.

Reading — No draws!

Now every match has a winner. If the score is 1–1, the teams play more minutes. This is extra time.

If the score is still 1–1, they take penalties. The team with more goals wins and goes to the next round.
Easy English — June 2026
  • ★ Can a match end 1–1 in this stage? Yes / No
  • ★★ What is "extra time"?
Answers
No — there is always a winner. Extra time = more minutes of play when the score is equal.

Grammar — "will" for predictions

I think … will win
Example
"I think Brazil will win."
"I think France will win 2–1."

Day 3 — England, Scotland, Wales…

Theme: the UK and its teams · Grammar: is / are
🎲 Move & speak — 2 games to start
1 · Human map — UK or not? — Give 5 pupils a label (England, Scotland, Wales, N. Ireland, Rep. of Ireland). They stand together if "in the UK", apart if not.
Republic of Ireland steps away — the key point.
2 · Vote with your feet — UK / not UK — Call a nation; pupils step to the UK side or the not-UK side. Quick rounds.
Pure recognition, fast pace.

Reading — One country, many teams

The United Kingdom (UK) is one country. But it has four football teams: England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland.

This is special — most countries have only one team!
Easy English — June 2026
  • ★ How many football teams does the UK have?
  • ★★ Name two of them.
Answers
Four. Any two of: England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland.

Grammar — is / are

Complete with "is" or "are"
England a football team.
England and Scotland in the UK.
London the capital.
Answers
is · are · is.

team · capital

Day 4 — What happened?

Theme: simple results · Grammar: past simple of "to win / to lose"
🎲 Move & speak — 2 games to start
1 · Running Dictation — short — Post 2–3 short sentences at the back wall. In pairs, the runner reads, memorises, comes back and dictates; the scribe writes. Swap halfway.
Use the model report's first lines.
2 · Human Sentence Building — easy — Hand out word cards for a simple result sentence ("France / won / the / match"). Pupils stand in the right order.
One short sentence is enough.

Day 5 — My favourite moment

Theme: looking back · Grammar: "My favourite … is/was"
🎲 Move & speak — 2 games to start
1 · Four Corners — favourites — Corners with a few remaining teams (your teacher names them). Pupils stand by their favourite and say "I think ___ will win."
Show the Round-of-16 teams first.
2 · Line-up — alphabetical teams — Pupils line up in alphabetical order of their favourite remaining team, asking each other in English ("What's your team?").
Movement + simple questions.

Speaking — Who is still playing?

Your teacher shows the teams that are still in. In pairs, say: "I think ___ will win the World Cup."
Setup
Show the remaining teams (Round of 16). Quick oral round-the-class.
