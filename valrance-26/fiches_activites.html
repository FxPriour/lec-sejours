Ballon Royal · activity language sheet

Séjour Valrance · run in English · ~2–3 min warm-up + reference vocabulary

What it is

A dodgeball-style team game: players throw soft balls to hit opponents and get them "out". Often one player is the protected King (or Queen) — if the King is hit, the team loses. Close to English dodgeball / "king of the court".

Vocabulary

to throw / to catch lancer / attraper
to dodge esquiver
to hit / to get hit toucher / être touché
to be out être éliminé
to aim (at) viser
to protect the King protéger le roi
a team / teammate une équipe / coéquipier
the line / the court la ligne / le terrain
safe / out of bounds sauf / hors-jeu
to win / to lose gagner / perdre

Useful phrases (to run the game)

For you & the players
"Get into two teams." · "Stay behind the line." · "If the ball hits you, you're out." · "Don't aim at the head!" · "Protect your King!" · "Catch it — you're back in!" · "Game over — well played!"
🎲 2-minute warm-up
Mime & guess. Call out an action (throw, catch, dodge, aim, hit); pupils mime it. Then reverse: a pupil mimes, the others shout the English verb. ★★★ Add a sentence: "I dodged the ball!"

Paintball Éponge · activity language sheet

Séjour Valrance · run in English · ~2–3 min warm-up + reference vocabulary

What it is

Like paintball, but with wet sponges instead of paint: teams throw soaked sponges to "tag" opponents. Soft, wet and very physical. English: "wet sponge tag" / sponge battle.

Vocabulary

a sponge une éponge
wet / soaked mouillé / trempé
to soak / to squeeze tremper / essorer
a bucket of water un seau d'eau
to throw / to tag lancer / toucher
to splash éclabousser
to take cover se mettre à l'abri
a hit / a point un touché / un point
the base / the zone la base / la zone
soaking wet trempé jusqu'aux os

Useful phrases (to run the game)

For you & the players
"Soak your sponge in the bucket." · "Throw below the shoulders." · "If you get tagged, run back to base." · "Watch out — take cover!" · "You're soaking wet!" · "One point for the red team!"
🎲 2-minute warm-up
Wet or dry? Quick word sort: call words (sponge, bucket, splash, dodge, towel…); pupils step left for "water words", right for "not water". ★★ Each pupil adds one more wet-weather word they know.

Capture the Flag · activity language sheet

Séjour Valrance · run in English · ~2–3 min warm-up + reference vocabulary · (name already in English)

What it is

Two teams, two territories. Each team hides a flag in its base; you must sneak into the other team's half, grab their flag and bring it back without being tagged. A classic outdoor strategy game.

Vocabulary

a flag un drapeau
a base / territory une base / un territoire
a border / a line une frontière / une ligne
to sneak / to creep se faufiler
to capture / to grab capturer / attraper
to tag / to catch toucher / attraper
a prisoner / jail un prisonnier / la prison
to free / to rescue libérer / secourir
to guard / a guard garder / un gardien
a strategy / a plan une stratégie / un plan

Useful phrases (to run the game)

For you & the players
"This is your territory; that's theirs." · "Don't cross the border yet." · "If you're tagged, go to jail." · "A teammate can free you." · "Guard the flag!" · "They've captured our flag — stop them!"
🎲 3-minute warm-up
Plan it in English. In small teams, give 90 seconds to agree a simple plan using will: "I will guard the flag.", "You will sneak round the side." Each team says its plan aloud. ★★★ Add a condition: "If they catch me, you will free me."

Secret Agent Challenge · activity language sheet

Séjour Valrance · run in English · ~2–3 min warm-up + reference vocabulary
⚠️ Règles supposées — je ne connais pas le détail de ce jeu maison. Je pars d'une interprétation « jeu de missions / déduction type espion » (missions secrètes, indices, démasquer un agent). Le vocabulaire reste utile ; ajuste les règles si besoin.

What it might be

A spy-themed game: players get secret missions or identities, follow clues, complete tasks discreetly, and try to unmask a hidden agent — or avoid being caught. (Think "spy mission" + "who is the impostor?".)

Vocabulary

a secret agent / a spy un agent secret / espion
a mission / a task une mission / une tâche
a clue un indice
a code / a password un code / mot de passe
to solve / to crack résoudre / déchiffrer
to hide / to spy on se cacher / espionner
a suspect un suspect
to unmask / to reveal démasquer / révéler
an identity / disguise une identité / déguisement
to complete a mission accomplir une mission

Useful phrases (to run the game)

For you & the players
"Here is your secret mission." · "Find the next clue." · "Don't reveal your identity." · "Who do you suspect?" · "I think the agent is…" · "Mission complete!"
🎲 3-minute warm-up
Who is the spy? One pupil leaves; the class picks a "secret word". The pupil returns and asks yes/no questions to guess it: "Is it an object? Is it in this room?" Recycles question forms before the real game. ★★ Limit to 5 questions.

Grille magique · activity language sheet

Séjour Valrance · run in English · ~2–3 min warm-up + reference vocabulary
⚠️ Règles supposées — nom maison. Je pars d'une interprétation « jeu de grille » : un quadrillage de cases à conquérir / cocher en réussissant des défis (type morpion géant, bingo de défis, ou grille à compléter). Ajuste si le jeu est différent.

What it might be

A grid game: a board of squares, each with a challenge or a number. Teams pick a square, complete the task, and claim it — first to line up a row (or to hold the most squares) wins. Like a giant noughts and crosses / challenge bingo.

Vocabulary

a grid / a board une grille / un plateau
a square / a cell une case
a row / a column une ligne / une colonne
a challenge / a task un défi / une tâche
to choose / to pick choisir
to claim a square remporter une case
to cross out / to tick barrer / cocher
in a row / to line up à la suite / aligner
a point / a score un point / un score
your turn / my turn à toi / à moi

Useful phrases (to run the game)

For you & the players
"Choose a square." · "What's the challenge?" · "Complete the task to claim it." · "Three in a row wins!" · "It's your turn." · "Cross it out / tick the box."
🎲 2-minute warm-up
Grid positions. Draw a 3×3 grid; number the squares. Call coordinates ("top left", "middle row", "bottom right"); pupils point or call the number. Pre-teaches the position language. ★★ Pupils give the next coordinate themselves.

Olympiades aquatiques · water games · activity language sheet

Séjour Valrance · Day 1 (Monday 29/06) · run in English · ~2–3 min warm-up + reference vocabulary

What it is

A team "olympics" of water games: relays and challenges using water — sponge relays, carrying water in cups, filling a bucket, water-balloon passes, etc. Teams score points across several events. English: water games / water olympics. First-day activity — a great low-pressure way to get pupils speaking and moving together.

Vocabulary

a team / a relay une équipe / un relais
an event / a round une épreuve / une manche
a bucket / a cup un seau / un gobelet
a sponge / a balloon une éponge / un ballon
to fill / to empty remplir / vider
to carry / to pass porter / passer
to spill / to splash renverser / éclabousser
to soak / wet tremper / mouillé
to win a point / to score gagner un point / marquer
first / second / third place 1ʳᵉ / 2ᵉ / 3ᵉ place
the finish line la ligne d'arrivée
Ready, steady, go! À vos marques, prêts, partez !

Useful phrases (to run the games)

For you & the players
"Get into your teams." · "Line up behind the start." · "Fill your cup and run to the bucket." · "Don't spill the water!" · "Pass the sponge to the next person." · "Ready, steady, go!" · "Red team wins this round — one point!"
🎲 2–3-minute warm-up
Team chant & verbs. First, each team invents a 4-word English chant ("Go, go, blue team!"). Then mime-relay: the first pupil mimes a water verb (fill, carry, pass, splash, empty), the next must name it in English before miming the next. ★★★ Add a result sentence afterwards: "We filled the bucket first!" (past simple — links to Day 4).
Because this is Day 1, keep it light: the goal is teams speaking English out loud, not perfect accuracy.
