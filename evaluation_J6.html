Britain is often described as a "green and pleasant land", but the reality is more complicated. The country has lost more than half of its biodiversity since the 1970s, and urban green spaces are disappearing at an alarming rate. Yet there is growing awareness. In 2023, the UK government launched a new initiative requiring local councils to create or restore green spaces in every major city by 2030.

Young people are at the forefront of this movement. School strikes, community gardens, and wildlife corridors in city parks are all signs of a generation that refuses to accept environmental decline. "We are the ones who will live with the consequences," said one 16-year-old activist at a rally in London last year. "So we are the ones who must act."

But not everyone is optimistic. Critics argue that government targets are too vague, funding too limited, and the timescale too long. Some scientists warn that by 2030, it may already be too late for certain species. The debate, it seems, is far from over.
Adapted from The Guardian / BBC News, 2024
