★ Reading — England's oldest recorded town (20 min)
Lis ce texte et réponds aux questions.
Colchester is England's oldest recorded town. It was the first Roman capital of Britain, known as Camulodunum. The Romans built a temple dedicated to Emperor Claudius here around AD 50. You can still walk through the original Roman walls today — they are the oldest in Britain.

The town has been shaped by many different peoples over the centuries: Romans, Anglo-Saxons, Normans and Victorians all left their mark. Colchester Castle, built by the Normans in the 11th century on the foundations of the Roman temple, is now one of the largest Norman keeps in Europe. The High Street still follows the original Roman road layout.

Today, Colchester is a growing university town with a thriving arts scene and a rich cultural life. It has changed a lot — but its history is everywhere you look.
Adapted from Visit Colchester / Colchester Museums
Comprehension questions :

J2 — Créneau 2 · Art gallery debrief & debate

Axe : La création et le rapport aux arts · Oral interaction + production écrite · Durée : ~2h
★ Gallery debrief (15 min)
En groupes de 3–4. Chacun parle d'une œuvre qu'il a remarquée pendant la visite. Structure :
SEE — THINK — WONDER (méthode de pensée visuelle)

★★ Task — Debate : "Should art be free?" (25 min)
En groupes de 4. Chaque membre a un rôle. Durée : 12 min de débat, 5 min de compte-rendu écrit.
🎙 Moderator
Introduce the topic, give the floor, manage time. Ask: "What do you think, [name]?" "Do you agree?" "Can you give an example?" ★★★ Also challenge: "But what about…?"
✅ For (art should be free)
Art belongs to everyone. Culture reduces inequality. Museums are funded by taxpayers already. Great art inspires society.
★ Use: "I think…" "In my opinion…"
❌ Against (art should not be free)
Artists need to earn a living. Quality costs money. Free entry reduces the value of art. Private funding is more efficient.
★★ Use: "I disagree because…" "On the other hand…"
📝 Secretary
Take notes on the main arguments. After the debate, you will write the 3 strongest arguments for each side.
